Roman Senninger

I am an Associate Professor of Political Science at Aarhus University.

My research centers on political elites, institutions, and public opinion in Europe. I am particularly interested in how politicians and voters select and use information for decision-making. Methodologically, I have a strong interest in experiments and computational social science.

I was educated at the University of Vienna and Aarhus University, and have held visiting positions at the London School of Economics and Political Science, Harvard University, the University of Zurich, and the University of Vienna.

Currently, I hold a three-year Semper Ardens Accelerate Grant from the Carlsberg Foundation alongside a NOVA grant from the Aarhus University Research Foundation.
